TL;DR

Rick Allen spent more than 16 years in Delphi, Indiana. Before his 2022 arrest, Rick was known as a devoted husband and father, a friendly face at the town's only pharmacy, and a regular at the local pub. By every measure, he was a stable and remarkably normal guy.

Now serving a 130-year sentence, the trial Court has finally begun to slowly release exhibits that a select group of 12 journalists were given a mere 15 minutes to inspect at the end of each trial day. With each limited release of exhibits, concern grows about the integrity of the investigation and the belief that Rick Allen was wrongfully convicted after a tragedy of errors continues to gain traction.

To the point breakdown of the evidence used against Rick and the issues plaguing the prosecution and trial court can be found here.

10,000 Foot Overview

Police ask for the public's help

New tip line announced

February 16, 2017
  • Police announce a new tipline and ask anyone who was at or near the Monon High Bridge trail on February 13, 2017 to please contact them to answer some questions so investigators can determine if they have any information that would assist their investigation.
  • Rick and Kathy Allen go to the Sheriff's Office to provide the limited information he may have from his short walk on the trail.
  • A woman takes his contact information and tells him someone will reach out to him soon.

Dan Dulin interview

February 18, 2017
  • When DNR Conservation Officer, Dan Dulin, contacts Rick to meet him and follow up on the information he provided to the Sheriff's Office, Rick is in the car on the way to the store.
  • Rick pulls over to speak with Dulin and offers to meet Dulin since he's already downtown.
  • Rick meets with Dulin for approximately 10 to 15 minutes in the Save-A-Lot parking lot.
  • Rick provided his driver's license and allowed Dulin to coolect all the information about his phone.
  • Rick tells Dulin he was at the trail sometime around noon and that he stayed for about an hour.
  • Dulin asks some personal questions ranging from family, hobbies, and what their usual routine was when visiting the trail.
  • Rick tells Dulin he's married with an adult daughter but no grandchildren, and that he likes to look at the fish on the bridge.
  • Rick tells Dulin that he crossed Freedom Bridge and saw a group of 3 girls on his way to the Monon High Bridge and doesn't remember a description but believes the one with dark hair was older than the other two.
  • Rick tells Dulin that after walking to to the bridge, he sat on a bench to watch his stock ticker and then left.
  • Rick said he may have seen other people at the trail but was distracted on his phone and didn't recall anyone else that stuck out to him while he was there but he mentions noticing an SUV and a sedan parked at the farmer's entrance when he was at the bench at the trail split before walking to his vehicle to go back home.

"Rick Allen Whiteman"

Kathy Shank's Investigation

September 21, 2022

Franklin Street Investigation Center

  • Kathy finds a lead sheet for "Rick Allen Whiteman" about a man who reported being on the trail and seeing 3 girls on February 13th, 2017.
  • The lead was marked 'cleared' but because Kathy didn't know who he was, she took it to Tony Liggett and presented it as if he was likely the same man a group of 4 girls who saw an unknown man on the trail on Februay 13th, 2017.
  • Tony Liggett and Steve Mullin contact Conservation Officer Dan Dulin about his interview with Rick in 2017 to ask for his notes and audio of the interview.
  • Dulin tells them he recorded all of his interviews but couldn't find any audio in his files for Rick Allen.
  • Dulin decides to look Rick up in his DNR database and sees Rick's fishing license renewal for April 2017 noted a 2-inch height change from 5 feet 4 inches to 5 feet 6 inches.
  • Dulin felt this change on Rick's fishing license renewal — a change which is 2-inches closer to Bridge Guy's height — could be a significant indicator that Rick Allen took steps after the murder to look less like Bridge Guy.
  • Dulin reports this bombshell lead to Liggett and Mullin who run off a gahtdamn cliff with it.

Mullin's Investigation

After September 21, 2022
  • Mullin decides to review the vehicles seen on Hoosier Harvest Store surveillance footage on February 13th, 2022 and after observing a dark colored hatchback or SUV, he takes Liggett to CVS to take pictures of his vehicle for comparison.
  • Believing the vehicle seen on Hoosier Harvest Store video matches Rick Allen's vehicle, Liggett and Mullin contact Rick and ask him to come in for an interview.

Liggett and Mullin's Interrogation

October 13, 2022

Franklin Street Investigation Center

  • Liggett and Mullin knock on Rick's door and tell him they're going back to the beginning of the Abby and Libby case and that they want to talk to him about information he provided on February 16, 2017.
  • Rick agrees to go with them to the police station and during the interview, he tells them that he believes he went to the trail around noon and stayed for roughly an hour before going home. When asked, Rick clarifies that 3:30 pm would have been way later than he was out there.
  • Rick tells them he parked near the old railroad bridge over State Road 25 and that he saw a group of 3 girls at the trail head when he first arrived.
  • When asked to elaborate on his prior trips to the trail, Rick tells them that their usual routine when visiting the trail is to cross Freedom Bridge and continue walking to High Bridge.
  • When redirected back to Dulin's narrative that Rick went out on the bridge, Rick tells them that it's been years since he crossed the entire bridge due to the bridge's decay, but that he loves to fish and usually made it out to the first trestle to look at the fish.
  • When Liggett and Mullin redirect Rick back to what he remembers about February 13th, 2017, Rick continues deferring to Dulin's report and tells them he sat on the bench at the trail split for a bit.
  • Rick tells them that while he was at the trail split, he noticed an SUV and a sedan were parked at the old farmer's entrance.
  • Rick tells them he may have seen some other people at the trail but oesn't remember seeing anyone that stuck out in his mind.
  • When asked what vehicle he drove that day and which route he took to park, Rick tells them he would have driven through town to get to the trail and that he doesn't remember which vehicle he drove that day.
  • When pressed, Rick tells them he could have driven either one of the vehicles the Allen's owned, but that back in 2017, the black Ford Focus was primarily Kathy's vehicle and that she even had her little cat license plates on it.
  • When asked what he was wearing that day, Rick couldn't remember but said he was probably wearing jeans, tennis shoes, and either a jacket or a sweatshirt similar to the one he was wearing during the interview.
  • When asked what color the jacket would have been, Rick said he believed it was black but could have been blue or black.
  • When asked to describe what other shoes he owned and could have potentially been wearing, Rick tells them he also had military boots, and a pair of work boots.
  • When asked if he could have been wearing a hat, Rick tells them that he always kept a skull cap in his jacket pocket so if he did wear a hat that day it would have been the skull cap.
  • When Liggett and Mullin's tone shifts, Rick asks for the interview to end.

Arrest

Jerry Holeman's Interrogation

October 26, 2022

Indiana State Police Post in Lafayette

  • Rick and Kathy Allen go to the Lafayette Police Post to pick up their vehicle. Jerry Holeman tells Rick he just has a few questions but the questions escalate into an "accidental" interrogation.
  • The interrogation was recorded but Holeman is never heard giving Rick his miranda warnings.
  • Rick denies any involvement in the murders and maintains his innocence for the entire duration of the interrogation, even when Holeman is screaming profanities at the top of his lungs.
  • After more than 2 hours of trying to wear Rick down, Holeman loses complete control of his emotions and takes Rick into custody despite not learning a single piece of new information than he had before Rick and Kathy arrived to pick up their vehiclle.

Kathy Allen retains Attorney Brett Gibson

October 27, 2022
  • Brett Gibson sends a letter to Carroll County Sheriff's Office telling them he's been retained to represent Rick and informing them that any attempts to question Rick should be terminated.
  • Prosecutor Dick McLeland files the probable cause affidavit, charging information, and a verified request to prohibit public access to those documents.

Police Announce Major Development

October 28, 2022
  • ISP announces a news conference has been scheduled for October 31, 2022 to discuss a major development in the 2017 murders of Libby German and Abby Williams.
  • FOX59 and CBS4 independently confirm, through sources, that a man was taken into custody in Carroll County in connection with the Delphi case. He is identified through jail records as 50-year-old Richard Allen.
  • After the initial hearing, Rick is transferred to White County Jail.

Safekeeping Order

November 1, 2022
  • Rick's handwritten letter asking for the Court to appoint Counsel while he was housed at the White County Jail is postmarked in Indianapolis.
November 2, 2022
  • Judge Benjamin Diener prepares a Request for a Sakeeping Order for Sheriff Tobe Leazenby to sign.
November 3, 2022
  • Judge Benjamin Diener grants the Request for a Safekeeping Order that he prepared for the Sheriff the day prior, and then recuses himself from the case.
  • Rick is transferred to Westville Correctional Facility and placed in solitary confinement in the Restrictive Housing Unit under 24-hour surveillance where he will be held for the next 2 years.
