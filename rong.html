Indiana v. Rick Allen

Rick

TL;DR

Rick Allen spent more than 16 years in Delphi, Indiana. Before his 2022 arrest, Rick was known as a devoted husband and father, a friendly face at the town's only pharmacy, and a regular at the local pub. By every measure, he was a stable and remarkably normal guy.

Now serving a 130-year sentence, the trial Court has finally begun to slowly release exhibits that a select group of 12 journalists were given a mere 15 minutes to inspect at the end of each trial day. With each limited release of exhibits, concern grows about the integrity of the investigation and the belief that Rick Allen was wrongfully convicted after a tragedy of errors continues to gain traction.

To the point breakdown of the evidence used against Rick and the issues plaguing the prosecution and trial court can be found here.

Abby and Libby reported missing

The search for the girls

February 13, 2017

High Bridge Trail

  • 1:49 pm — Kelsi German drops Libby and Abby off at the High Bridge trail.
  • 2:05 pm — Libby posts a photo of the empty Monon High Bridge followed by a photo of Abby crossing the empty bridge at 2:07 pm to her Snapchat Story.
  • 2:13 pm — While taking a video of Abby crossing the bridge for the first time, Libby captures a man in the background crossing the bridge behind Abby.
  • 3:14 pm — Derrick arrives at the trail but finds no sign of the girls and begins calling family around 3:30 pm to help track the girls down.
  • 5:20 pm — After pleas from family asking if anyone has seen or heard from the girls make the rounds on social media to no avail, Mike Patty formally reports the girls missing.
  • 9:30 pm — First responders from the police department, sheriff's office, fire department, and department of natural resources join the official search.
  • The official search is called off around midnight when the Sheriff tells the public there won't be an Amber Alert as they have no information that the girls are in any danger, but friends and family continue searching through the night.
February 14, 2017

High Bridge Trail

  • 8:00 am — Hundreds of volunteers show up to help search for Abby and Libby.
  • 12:15 pm — Two bodies are discovered across the creek on the private property of Ron Logan approximately 1,000 yards from the bridge.
  • 2:15 pm — Police announce they are now investigating this as a double homicide, and they do suspect foul play.

    Kathy Shank's Investigation

    September 21, 2022

    Franklin Street Investigation Center

    • Kathy finds a lead sheet for "Rick Allen Whiteman" about a man who reported being on the trail and seeing 3 girls on February 13th, 2017.
    • The lead was marked 'cleared' but because Kathy didn't know who he was, she took it to Tony Liggett and presented it as if he was likely the same man a group of 4 girls who saw an unknown man on the trail on Februay 13th, 2017.
    • Tony Liggett and Steve Mullin contact Conservation Officer Dan Dulin about his interview with Rick Allen in 2017 to ask for his notes and audio of the interview but despite telling them he recorded all of his interviews, Dulin couldn't find any audio in his files for Rick Allen.

    Dan Dulin's Investigation

    September 21, 2022

    Who Knows

    • After he was contacted by Liggett and Mullin and not being able to find any audio for his interview with Rick Allen, Dulin decided to look Rick up in his DNR database and sees Rick's fishing license renewal April 2017 showed a 2 inch height change.
    • Despite the fact that Dulin didn't think Rick Allen purportedly saying he was at the trail from 1:30 to 3:30 pm the day the girls went missing was significant enough to contact Liggett and Mullin, Dulin felt this change on Rick's fishing license of 2 inches — closer to Bridge Guy's height — could be a significant indicator that Rick Allen took steps after the murder to look less like Bridge Guy.
    • Dulin immediately reported this bombshell lead to Liggett and Mullin.

    Mullin's Investigation

    After September 21, 2022
    • After Liggett and Mullin receive the bombshell lead about Rick Allen changing his height on his fishing licesne renewal closer to the height of Bridge Guy, Mullin decides to review the vehicles seen on Hoosier Harvest Store surveillance footage on February 13th, 2022 and observes a dark colored hatchback or SUV.
    • Liggett and Mullin contact Rick to ask for an interview.

    Liggett and Mullin's Interrogation

    October 13, 2022

    Franklin Street Investigation Center

    • Liggett and Mullin knock on Rick's door and tell him they're going back to the beginning of the Abby and Libby case and that they want to talk to him about information he provided on February 16, 2017.
    • Rick agrees to go with them to the police station and during the interview, he tells them that he believes he went to the trail around noon and stayed for roughly an hour before going home. When asked, Rick clarifies that 3:30 pm would have been way later than he was out there.
    • Rick tells them he parked near the old railroad bridge over State Road 25 and that he saw a group of 3 girls at the trail head when he first arrived
    • When asked to elaborate on his prior trips to the trail, Rick tells them that their usual routine when visiting the trail is to cross Freedom Bridge and continue walking to High Bridge.
    • When redirected back to Dulin's narrative that Rick went out on the bridge, Rick tells them that it's been years since he crossed the entire bridge due to the bridge's decay, but that he loves to fish and usually made it out to the first trestle to look at the fish.
    • When Liggett and Mullin redirect Rick back to what he remembers about February 13th, 2017, Rick defers to Dulin's report but tells them he sat on the bench at the trail split for a bit.
    • Rick tells them that while he was at the trail split, he noticed an SUV and a sedan were parked at the old farmer's entrance.
    • Rick tells them that he doesn't remember seeing anyone else on his walk back to his vehicle to head home.
    • When asked what vehicle he drove that day and which route he took to park, Rick tells them he would have driven through town to get to the trail and that he doesn't remember which vehicle he drove that day.
    • When pressed, Rick tells them he could have driven either vehicle the Allen's owned but that back in 2017, the black Ford Focus was primarily Kathy's vehicle and that she even had her little cat license plates on it.
    • When asked what he was wearing that day, Rick couldn't remember but said he was probably wearing jeans, tennis shoes, and either a jacket or a sweatshirt similar to the one he was wearing during the interview.
    • When asked what color the jacket would have been, Rick said he believed it was black but could have been blue or black.
    • When asked to describe what other shoes he owned and could have potentially been wearing, Rick tells them he also had military boots, and a pair of work boots.
    • When asked if he could have been wearing a hat, Rick tells them that he always kept a skull cap in his jacket pocket so if he did wear a hat that day it would have been the skull cap.
    • When Liggett and Mullin's tone shifts, Rick asks for the interview to end.

    Jerry Holeman's Interrogation

    October 26, 2022

    Indiana State Police Post in Lafayette

    • Rick and Kathy Allen go to the Lafayette Police Post to pick up their vehicle. Jerry Holeman tells Rick he just has a few questions but the questions escalate into an "accidental" interrogation.
    • The interrogation was recorded and Holeman is never heard giving Rick his miranda warnings.
    • Despite Holeman berating, threatening, and lying to Rick for almost 2 hours, Rick denies any involvement in the murders and maintains his innocence.
    • After more than 2 hours of trying to wear Rick down, Holeman loses control of his emotions and takes Rick into custody despite learning a single piece of new information let alone evidence than he had before Rick and Kathy arrived to pick up their vehiclle.
